About Staten Island

Staten Island is one of the five boroughs of New York City, often called the most overlooked — yet it is one of the most unique. Known for its waterfront views, green spaces, historic neighborhoods, and strong sense of community, Staten Island offers a slower, more reflective side of NYC.

It is home to beaches, forests, historic towns, and panoramic views of the Manhattan skyline. Unlike the crowded streets of Manhattan, Staten Island blends urban life with nature, giving it a peaceful yet powerful character.

Staten Island skyline at night — calm, quiet, unforgettable.

History

Staten Island has a rich and complex history dating back to Indigenous Lenape tribes, followed by Dutch and British settlement. It officially became part of New York City in 1898.

Historically, Staten Island was shaped by shipping, manufacturing, and immigration. Many families who settled here built tight-knit communities that still exist today. The borough played an important role in maritime trade and defense throughout American history.

Fun Facts

  • The Staten Island Ferry is completely free and runs 24/7.
  • Staten Island has more parkland than any other NYC borough.
  • The Statue of Liberty can be seen clearly from Staten Island’s shores.
  • It was once almost its own independent city.

Pop Culture

Staten Island has influenced music, film, and television. It is famously associated with the Wu-Tang Clan, whose legacy shaped hip-hop culture worldwide.

The borough has also been featured in movies and shows that highlight its raw, authentic atmosphere — far from the glamorous image of Manhattan.
