History & Culture

Staten Island, originally called Richmond, is the least populated borough of New York City but one of the most historic. It was first settled by the Dutch in the 1600s and later became a key gateway for immigrants arriving in America.

Unlike the fast pace of Manhattan, Staten Island is known for its quiet neighborhoods, waterfront views, and strong community culture. It blends suburban calm with city identity.
