Welcome to Staten Island

A night-city borough filled with history, culture, and identity

Introduction

Staten Island is one of the five boroughs of New York City, located in the southwest of the city. It is often described as the calmest borough, offering a balance between urban life and peaceful residential areas. At night, Staten Island reveals a breathtaking skyline and a quiet charm that sets it apart from the rest of New York City.

History

Before European settlers arrived, Staten Island was home to the Lenape Native American tribe. In the 1600s, the Dutch and later the British colonized the area. In 1898, Staten Island officially became one of the five boroughs of New York City. Today, the borough preserves its history through museums, landmarks, and historic towns.

Culture & Daily Life

Staten Island is known for its strong sense of community and family life. Many residents have lived there for generations. The borough is culturally diverse, influenced by Italian, Irish, African American, and Asian communities. Life on Staten Island moves at a slower pace compared to Manhattan, making it ideal for families and students.
